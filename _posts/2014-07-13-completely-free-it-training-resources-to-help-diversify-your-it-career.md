# Scope and overview

<span style="color: black">Inspired by <a href="https://twitter.com/concentrateddon/">Don Jones</a>&#8216; excellent &#8220;<a href="http://donjones.com/2014/07/12/dont-get-stuck-in-your-job/">Don&#8217;t Get Stuck in Your Job</a>&#8221; blog post, I&#8217;ve taken some time to compile a bunch of online IT training to help people diversify their IT knowledge, for free.<br /></span>

<span style="color: black">This post covers a selection of free IT training that I&#8217;ve found on my travels. Topics are diverse, and include areas like: Virtualization and Cloud, Networking, Security, Linux, Storage systems, PowerShell, to Programming and Database fundamentals.<br /></span>

<span style="color: black"><span style="color: black">I believe that as IT Professionals, the more aspects of IT that we understand and appreciate, the better we&#8217;re able to meet the needs of business. I&#8217;d really like this to evolve into a community resource for anyone who wants to diversify their IT knowledge.</span> If you&#8217;ve found other free IT training or resources that aren&#8217;t mentioned here, please feel free to mention it in the comments and I&#8217;ll endeavour to add it (I&#8217;m particularly interested in official training from vendors).<br /></span>

If you&#8217;re left wondering where on earth you&#8217;re going to get time to study all this, remember that it only takes about [20 hours to pick up a subject/skill](http://www.kabri.uk/2014/06/24/thoughts-on-learning-procrastination-and-the-myth-of-10000-hours/ "Thoughts on learning-procrastination, and the myth of 10,000 hours"), and that&#8217;s merely a month of Lunch-times!

                            ## NetApp
                            
                            <span style="color: black">NetApp have an incredible range of <a href="https://learningcenter.netapp.com/content/public/production/learning_maps/customer/lm_customer_t1.html">Web-Based courses</a> available for free, but you will need to register for an account (Choose a Guest account if you&#8217;re not a customer). Also, be careful with &#8220;free email addresses&#8221;. NetApp seem to verify your account and if it comes from a hotmail or gmail account, will not allow it access. I find this a slight aberration, as they allow access for Guests who don&#8217;t have NetApp systems, who could be future paying customers. I had success with an @outlook.com account, but your mileage may vary.<br /></span>
